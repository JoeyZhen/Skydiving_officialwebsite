Before Skydiving

WHAT TO BRING & WEAR

For your tandem skydive please remember you must bring a valid form of ID (driver's license or passport) and the credit card you paid with online. Wear comfortable, athletic clothes and closed shoes like sneakers.

Suitting up

Harness: Equipment worn by student around the legs and torso which attaches to the instructor via four (4) points of attachment, two (2) on the shoulders, and two (2) on the hips. The skydive center has jumpsuits designed to keep your clothes clean upon landing, and for hot days there are jump pants available. If you would like to wear a suit or pants, your instructor can direct you to the appropriate size. With your pockets empty and shoes on tight, your instructor or qualified staff member will help you into the harness. It may feel loose, this is on purpose for your comfort, final tightening adjustments will be made while attaching to your instructor. Your goggles will be either attached to the harness for later, or you may wear them around your neck. The instructor will tell you when to put the goggles on, wearing them too early can cause them to fog up, so wait for the instructors que. Properly fitting the goggles to your face is important to make the most of your experience. The key to proper fitting goggles is a good seal along the bottom edges, around the cheeks and over the nose. To do this, you may need to lower the goggles until a good seal is achieved. Tighten the goggles by pulling the knot of the strap by each temple. They should be very snug, but not uncomfortable.

Boarding the aircraft

It is important that you remain next to your instructor when crossing the fencing onto the active airport tarmac. Our instructors will maintain positive control, meaning they will hold on to your harness while escorting you to the airplane. In most cases, you will be boarding the plane while the engine is running and propeller is spinning. The propeller spins at a rate which makes it almost invisible, so it is extremely important to stay behind the wings at all times. We approach the aircraft from the rear, and your instructor will tell you where to sit.
